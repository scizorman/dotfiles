# RFI Section Catalog

This document lists all possible sections for an RFI. Not every section is required for every RFI. Select sections based on the specific procurement context and the information the issuer needs to gather.

## 1. Cover Page

Include the RFI title, issuing organization name, issue date, RFI reference number (for tracking), and response deadline.

---

## 2. Introduction and Purpose

### 2.1 Purpose of the RFI

Clearly state the purpose of the RFI. Always include a statement that the RFI is for information-gathering purposes and does not constitute a commitment to purchase.

What to include:

- The objective of the RFI (market research, vendor identification, solution exploration, etc.)
- Where this RFI sits in the procurement process (information gathering prior to a potential RFP)
- A disclaimer that responding to the RFI does not guarantee a contract or purchase

### 2.2 Confidentiality

Notes on information handling. State the confidentiality expectations for the RFI content itself and how vendor responses will be treated.

---

## 3. Organization Overview

Provide basic information about the issuing organization so that vendors can tailor their responses appropriately.

What to include:

- Organization name, industry, and business overview
- Organization size (headcount, number of locations, revenue range, etc. — to the extent relevant)
- Relevant departments or stakeholders involved

---

## 4. Background and Current State

Describe the context that led to issuing the RFI.

What to include:

- Overview of current operations or systems (as needed)
- Challenges or pain points being faced
- Reasons for considering improvement or change

Detailed requirements are not necessary here. Provide enough information for vendors to understand the general direction of what the organization is looking for.

---

## 5. Scope of Interest

Define the areas where information is being sought. Unlike an RFP, this does not need to be a detailed requirements specification — instead, indicate the direction and boundaries of what is being explored.

What to include:

- Product or service categories of interest
- Anticipated scope of deployment (target departments, approximate number of users, etc.)
- Technical prerequisites or constraints, if any (e.g., integration requirements with existing systems)

---

## 6. Information Requested from Vendors

The core section of the RFI. Present the questions for vendors in a structured, categorized format to facilitate comparison across responses.

### 6.1 Company Profile

- Company overview (year founded, headquarters, employee count)
- Primary business areas
- Financial stability information (optional)

### 6.2 Solution Overview

- Overview of products or services offered
- Key features and differentiators
- High-level technical architecture (if applicable)
- Deployment models (cloud, on-premises, hybrid, etc.)

### 6.3 Experience and References

- Track record with similar industries or organizations of comparable size
- Representative case studies
- Years of experience in the relevant domain

### 6.4 Capability and Approach

- High-level approach to addressing the stated challenges
- Customization and integration capabilities
- Product roadmap and planned feature enhancements

### 6.5 Support and Service

- Implementation and onboarding support
- Post-deployment support model (hours of operation, SLA overview)
- Training and education programs available

### 6.6 Security and Compliance

- Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
- Data residency and protection policies
- Compliance with industry-specific regulations

### 6.7 General Pricing Model

Specific quotes should be requested at the RFQ stage. Here, ask only for an overview of the pricing structure.

- Licensing model (subscription, usage-based, perpetual license, etc.)
- Whether initial setup costs apply and a rough range
- Key factors that influence pricing

---

## 7. Response Format and Instructions

Provide instructions to ensure vendor responses are comparable and easy to evaluate.

What to include:

- Required response format (Word, PDF, Excel response sheet, etc.)
- Response structure (respond in section order, match question numbering, etc.)
- Page or length guidelines (optional)
- Submission method (email, portal, etc.)
- Submission deadline
- Contact information for questions (point of contact, question submission deadline)

---

## 8. Timeline and Process

Outline the overall RFI process schedule.

What to include:

- RFI issue date
- Deadline for vendor questions
- Response submission deadline
- Evaluation period
- Next steps (shortlist notification, expected RFP issuance date, demo invitations, etc.)

---

## 9. Terms and Conditions

State the legal and procedural conditions governing the RFI.

What to include:

- The RFI does not constitute a commitment to purchase (reiterate)
- The issuer reserves the right to cancel or modify the RFI process at any time
- Costs incurred in responding are borne by each vendor
- Intellectual property treatment of response content
- Confidentiality obligations

---

## 10. Appendices

Attach supplementary materials as needed.

- Glossary (if industry-specific terminology is used)
- System architecture diagram (overview level)
- Excerpts from relevant policies or standards
- Response template (e.g., Excel spreadsheet)
